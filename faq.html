In general, we don’t want to take x-rays when they are not necessary. We’d prefer to not subject patients to needless radiation. If you have x-rays from a previous dental office, you can request to have them sent to our email - info@integrativedentistry.com. We actually prefer them! In the event that the x-rays are too old (typically older than 12 to 18 months), or the doctor needs to diagnose something that they can or can’t see, they may request we take new x-rays at your appointment.


To better understand our holistic diagnostic criteria and when x-rays are deemed necessary, you can review the ADA's Caries Risk Assessment Framework.


Take heart in that our x-ray machines are all digital, and that means a considerably less amount of radiation than older machines. In fact, there’s more radiation involved in your daily activities, such as eating, going outdoors, or even breathing, than a single x-ray in our office.

Yes, we use digital x-rays which are safer and more efficient than traditional film x-rays. They reduce radiation exposure by up to 90% and provide instant images for better diagnosis.

We completely agree with the American Dental Association’s updated guidelines! The ADA confirms that X-rays should never be a "one-size-fits-all" routine, and they should only be ordered when clinically necessary. That aligns perfectly with our conservative approach to dentistry. We strictly follow the ALARA principle—As Low As Reasonably Achievable—meaning we limit your exposure to the absolute minimum required.


So, when are they necessary for us? Because we view your oral health as fundamentally linked to your overall health, we cannot afford to guess about what is happening beneath the surface. Dental X-rays allow us to diagnose disease and damage that is simply not visible during a standard visual exam.


Without imaging, hidden issues like asymptomatic chronic infections, jawbone changes, or deep decay could go undetected and silently burden your immune system. By using ultra-low radiation digital imaging only when clinically justified, we gather the essential diagnostic information needed to ensure your mouth is truly healthy and biocompatible with the rest of your body. We will always discuss the need for any images with you beforehand so we can make informed decisions about your care together.


After your initial exam and x-rays, the frequency of future x-rays is determined by your provider using the ADA's Caries Risk Assessment Framework. Please note, x-rays may be taken at lesser intervals if there are new signs of decay, infection, or other symptoms that lead your provider to needing updated imaging.
